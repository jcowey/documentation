    public class StringSearchFacet.SubClause
extends StringSearchFacet.SearchClause
    The SubClause class models any SearchClause that in the first instance consists of more than one SearchTerm, SubClause objects stand in a HAS-A relationship with their constituent SearchClause parts via the clauseComponents ArrayList. In principle, these parts may be either SearchTerms or other SubClauses themselves in a fully recursive manner. However, because nested search-string parsing is not yet supported, the elements of the clauseComponents list will in fact always be SearchTerms. Note should be taken of the qualification 'in the first instance' found in the opening sentence, as processing will often reduce what was submitted as a multi-term search clause to a single term. In particular, some kinds of complex searches (namely character-proximity, word-proximity-with-leading-wildcards, and negative assertion) are converted to single-term regex representations for the purposes of querying Solr.
